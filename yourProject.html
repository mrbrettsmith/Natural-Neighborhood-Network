Make a Statement with your Yard

Your garden is a great way to get to know your neighborhood. The care and effort you put into gardening not only beautifies the street, it helps keep the city healthy. An intentional garden supports nature while letting you make an eye-catching statement because when your garden gets attention it also helps spread awareness about sustainable gardening practices. The Natural Neighborhood Network was created to help you make your mark.

What kind of Garden is Right for you?

Even a tiny space can have a big impact. A small garden made with care can support pollinators, clean the water, and be the foundation that other flora and fauna need to thrive. The Natural Neighborhood Network has focused on some of the most impactful sustainable projects for Minneapolis: Rain Gardens, Native Prairie Wildflowers, Pollinator Habitats, and Urban Tree Coverage. Each of these themes is designed to restore native plants which helps the immediate area, as well as the entire region, be healthy and resistant to weather events.

Choosing the right project for your space can be difficult. We are here to help: We host resources, expert advice, and inspiration. From plant selections to maintenance, from skill sharing to grant funding, we have collected the best local tools and knowledge to get your sustainable garden started. If you are having problems selecting a theme for your first project, don't worry! The Natural Neighborhood Network is designed to grow along with your garden, the signage expanding as you improve and develop different initiatives.

Connect with Neighbors and Friends

Gardening is fun, and brings life to your street. A beautiful yard brings joy and sparks curiosity for your neighbors. It also helps spread awareness of the good impact a garden has on the community and inspires others to start projects of their own. The Natural Neighborhood Network’s mission is to help connect individual gardeners to others in their neighborhood.

By adding signage to your garden it makes our community's efforts visible. Linking projects in the area is a celebration of the care and creativity of neighbors working together. A sustainable project may seem small on its own, but when it is grouped with others it does more than inspire, it provides a vital network of rest-stops for animals and plants. It mitigates wastewater and cools the urban environment. Of course, it also makes your neighborhood look beautiful!

Solutions for Renters and Homeowners

The Natural Neighborhood Network is for everyone in our city. The more neighbors who participate the bigger the impact we will have. Gardening is great exercise and stress relief, and a vibrant property is great at getting smiles. Homeowners can bring interest to their property and express their personality. Renters can use garden beds, window boxes, and boulevards to get engaged. The Natural Neighborhood Network’s partners have tips for approaching a landlord or community living area with a sustainable garden proposal: often the conversations lead others joining in to help the project!

Ready to Get Involved?

If you are interested in becoming part of the Natural Neighborhood Network, we would love to have you as part of our community! If you have an existing project and would like to get interpretive signage please specify what sign components you are interested in. All signage is made locally from recycled materials, and can be mailed or picked up at one of our partner nurseries.

For new projects, we offer advice and a starter kit with signage and instructions. We look forward to seeing your garden bloom!

We encourage everyone to add their projects to our neighborhood map. Like the signage, it connects our community and is the perfect way to show your passion for sustainability.
